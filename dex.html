Introduction to KCCA Club

KCCA Club, also known as Kampala Capital City Authority Club, is a Ugandan professional football club based in Kampala, the capital city of Uganda. The club was founded in 2012 and is owned by the Kampala Capital City Authority (KCCA), the city’s governing body. KCCA Club is one of the most successful football clubs in Uganda, having won several league titles and domestic cups.

History of KCCA Club

KCCA Club was formed in 2012 after the merger of two Kampala-based football clubs, KCC FC and Express FC. The new club was named KCCA FC, and it took over the league position of KCC FC in the top-tier Ugandan football league. The merger aimed to create a strong football club that would represent the city of Kampala and compete at the national and regional levels. Since its formation, KCCA Club has been one of the dominant forces in Ugandan football. The club has won several league titles, including five consecutive titles between 2016 and 2020. KCCA Club has also won several domestic cups, including the Ugandan Cup and Super Cup. The club’s success has been attributed to its strong management, talented players, and modern training facilities.

Management and Ownership

KCCA Club is owned by the Kampala Capital City Authority (KCCA), which is responsible for governing and managing the city of Kampala. The club’s management team is led by a board of directors appointed by KCCA. The current CEO of KCCA Club is Anisha Muhoozi, who has been in office since 2019. Under her leadership, the club has continued to achieve success on the pitch while also focusing on community development and social responsibility initiatives.

Stadium and Training Facilities

KCCA Club plays its home matches at the StarTimes Stadium, which is located in Lugogo, a suburb of Kampala. The stadium has a capacity of 10,000 spectators and features modern facilities such as VIP seats, a media center, and modern changing rooms. The club also has access to several training facilities, including the Philip Omondi Stadium, which is located in Kisenyi, a suburb of Kampala. The training facilities are equipped with modern equipment and are used by the club’s various teams for training and matches.

Players and Coaching Staff

KCCA Club has a talented squad of players who have helped the club achieve success on the pitch. The club’s coaching staff is led by head coach Morley Byekwaso, who has been in charge since 2021. The club’s medical team is led by Dr. Arafat Abdullatif, who ensures that the players are in good health and fit to play.
