KCCA Board Management: Overview and Responsibilities

The Kampala Capital City Authority (KCCA) is a public corporation in Uganda, responsible for providing strategic and operational guidance to the city of Kampala. The KCCA board is a crucial element of the organization’s governance structure, overseeing its management, administration, and operations. Here’s a detailed overview of the KCCA board management, its members, roles, and responsibilities.

KCCA Board Composition

The KCCA board comprises 11 members, including the Executive Director (Ex-officio), who serves as the board’s secretary. The other members include: Authorized Representative of the Minister of Finance Authorized Representative of the Minister of Local Government Authorized Representative of the Minister responsible for Physical Planning A representative from the private sector (nominated by the business community) A representative from the civil society (nominated by an umbrella body) A representative from the youth (nominated by an umbrella body) A representative from the academic and research community (nominated by an umbrella body) Three representatives from the divisions of Nakawa, Makindye, and Rubaga (nominated by their respective division councils)
